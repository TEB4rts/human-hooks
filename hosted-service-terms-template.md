# Hosted service terms template (starter only, not legal advice)

Use this as a starting point for a lawyer-reviewed hosted offering.

## Key clauses you likely want

- service provided "as available" and "as is"
- no warranty of uninterrupted availability
- limits on indirect, consequential, punitive, or special damages
- cap on direct liability (often fees paid in prior 12 months)
- customer responsibility for review policies and business decisions
- customer responsibility for reviewing high-risk actions before execution
- explicit prohibition on relying on the software as sole legal, medical, or safety authority
- security/disclosure terms
- acceptable-use terms
- IP ownership and feedback license
- termination rights
- governing law / venue

## Sample language concepts

### Human review responsibility
The customer is responsible for configuring approval thresholds, validation policies, reviewer roles, and operational controls suitable for the customer's use case.

### No sole-source safety guarantee
The service is a workflow and decision-support tool and does not guarantee correctness, legality, safety, or regulatory compliance of any automated or human-approved action.

### Liability cap
To the maximum extent permitted by law, provider liability shall not exceed the fees paid by customer for the service during the 12 months preceding the claim.

### Excluded damages
In no event shall provider be liable for indirect, incidental, special, consequential, exemplary, or punitive damages, including lost profits, lost data, business interruption, or reputational harm.

## Important

Do not copy-paste this and assume you are done. Get a lawyer to review it for your jurisdiction and business model.
